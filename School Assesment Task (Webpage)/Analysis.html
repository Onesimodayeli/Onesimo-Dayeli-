Here we see that 51% of the participants do not agree that online transactions are safe. But if we take a further look into the access database underneath we can see that some of the participants (participants with ID 23,26, 32, 35, 38, 40, 41) who do agree have not taken further steps to protect their data even while agreeing t hat the internet is safe. And some of the participants like the participant with ID 29 agrees that the internet is safe but still has discussed website cookies mining data with her bank.

If we take a look at the bar graph above we can observe that there is an equal number of yes and no answers in the number of people that answered. This tells us that the chances are high that you could be a victim to a scam or privacy breach.

With the clustered bar graph, we see the significant difference between the amount of people who have or have not secured their financial data. This may also be the reason why the participants are victims of scams. We see that participants with ID 3, 21, 32, 36,38, 40 are victims of scams but they have taken steps to protect their financial data. While participants with ID 12, 15, 23, 26, 27, 29, 31, 33, 35, 37, 41, 42, 43, 45, 46 have not tried contacting their bank for extra cybersecurity.
